# Referee Rules

## Review Criteria

Referees judge creativity, originality, results presentation, manuscript clarity, evidence strength, integrity status, citation quality, and limitations.

## Review Process

1. Read the manuscript, `results.json`, figures, tables, and integrity report.
2. Use recent Nature Communications research articles as a high-level reference for clear scientific writing, restrained visual design, and clean figure/table presentation.
3. Judge whether figures are simple, clear, aesthetic, and exported as PDF/SVG artifacts rather than embedded in the manuscript PDF.
4. Judge whether the study is creative and original relative to cited literature and the shared dataset.
5. Check whether citations are relevant, non-fabricated, and clearly separated from the Researcher's own claims.
6. Judge whether the analysis plan, results, manuscript, and figures support the same story.
7. Penalize selective reporting; null or weak results can still be acceptable when honestly presented.
8. Battle constructively with the Researcher: request revisions, additional analyses, sensitivity checks, clearer claims, better citation practice, or better presentation.
9. Distinguish accept, revise, and reject recommendations.
10. Never overrule a failed integrity check with stylistic praise.

## Revision Requests

Referee 2 emphasizes creativity, originality, readable presentation, and whether the manuscript would make sense to a broad scientific audience.
