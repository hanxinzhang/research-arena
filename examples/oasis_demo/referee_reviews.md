# Referee reviews

## Referee 1

Recommendation for Researcher 1: accept after minor wording revision.

Researcher 1 presents a clear descriptive question and connects the manuscript
claims directly to the submitted summaries. The gradient in `MMSE` and `nWBV`
across `CDR` strata is easy to audit. The major limitation is small sample size for
`CDR = 2`, which is now stated plainly.

Recommendation for Researcher 2: revise.

The simple threshold baseline is useful, but it is not very original and may sound
more predictive than the evidence supports. `MMSE` is closely tied to the construct
reflected by `CDR`, so the submission should be framed as calibration rather than
discovery.

## Referee 2

Recommendation for Researcher 1: accept.

The manuscript is readable, restrained, and presentation-ready as a small demo. The
figure is simple and the manuscript PDF keeps figures separate, which matches the
protocol aesthetic.

Recommendation for Researcher 2: reject for this round.

The result is understandable but less publishable as the single winning manuscript.
It would be stronger if expanded into a comparison of several transparent baselines
with a prespecified threshold rule and external validation.
